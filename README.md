# OncoTrend Pro | Master Clinical Analytics Guide

OncoTrend Pro is an advanced, Hadoop-powered diagnostic visualization platform designed to bridge the gap between complex Big Data analytics and actionable clinical insight.

---

## 🧭 Master Interpretation Guide (Non-Medical)

This guide is designed for users without a medical background to help them understand the sophisticated statistical outputs generated by our Hadoop processing engine.

### 1. The Core Philosophy: "Statistical Footprinting"
Rather than giving a simple "Yes/No" answer, OncoTrend Pro looks at your data as a **Clinical Footprint**. We compare your measurements against thousands of historical cases stored in our **HDFS (Hadoop Distributed File System)** simulated layer.

#### The Radar Chart: Visualizing Similarity
The Radar Chart allows you to see multiple health markers at once. 
- **Center Point**: Zero value.
- **Outer Edges**: High values.
- **The Comparison**: If your colored area mimics the shape of the "Malignant" baseline, it means your physiological markers are following a pattern statistically similar to historical disease cases.

### 2. The Power of Z-Scores (σ-dev)
Medical data comes in different units (mmHg for blood pressure, mgs/dl for glucose). To compare them fairly, we use the **Z-Score**.

**Z-Score Formula (Simplified):**  
`Result = (Your Value - Population Average) / Standard Deviation`

| Z-Score (σ-dev) | Significance | Meaning in Plain English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0.0 - 1.0** | **Green: Typical** | Your value is very close to the average. This is what we expect to see in most healthy individuals. |
| **1.0 - 2.5** | **Yellow: Deviating** | Your value is starting to drift away from the average. It is "outside the norm" but not yet rare. |
| **2.5 - 5.0+** | **Red: Critical** | Your value is extremely rare. Statistically, less than 1% of the typical population has a value this far from the mean. |

## 📋 Clinical Glossary & Feature Guide

Understanding what you are entering is the first step toward better insight.

### 🩸 Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
*Analyzes the filtering efficiency of your renal system.*
- **Serum Creatinine (SC)**: A waste product from muscle wear and tear. **High levels** indicate the kidneys are struggling to filter blood.
- **Blood Urea (BU)**: Measures nitrogen in the blood. Like creatinine, high BU levels point toward decreased kidney function.
- **Blood Glucose (BGR)**: High sugar levels are a leading cause of long-term kidney damage (Diabetic Nephropathy).

### 🎗️ Breast Cancer (Diagnostic)
*Analyzes physical characteristics of cell nuclei from biopsies.*
- **Radius Mean**: The average distance from the center of the cell nucleus to its perimeter.
- **Texture Mean**: Measures the "roughness" of the cell surface. Malignant cells often have more irregular textures.
- **Area Mean**: Simply how much space the cell nucleus takes up. Larger, swollen nuclei are often signifiers of rapid division.

### 👩‍⚕️ Cervical Cancer (Risk Factors)
*Analyzes behavioral and biological markers linked to oncology.*
- **Smoking Years**: Correlates strongly with DNA damage in cervical tissue.
- **Number of Pregnancies**: A biological marker that can influence hormonal environments relevant to risk.

## 🛠️ Usage Scenario (Step-by-Step)

1. **Selection**: Choose "Chronic Kidney Disease Analysis".
2. **Input**: Enter a Serum Creatinine of `4.5` (Note the range is typically `0.5 - 1.2`).
3. **Execution**: Click "Generate Clinical Profile".
4. **Analysis**: 
    - The system calculates a Z-Score of ~3.0 for Creatinine.

    - The Radar chart will show a massive spike toward the Malignant baseline, visually warning you of the deviation.

## 🏗️ Technical Architecture (The Hadoop Engine)

### Data Tiering
1. **Raw (Ingestion)**: Original UCI CSVs reside in `hdfs/user/hadoop/raw/`.
2. **Processing (Spark)**: [analytics.py](file:///Users/pushkarajjoshi/Projects/Cancer%20Risk%20Trend%20Checker%20using%20Hadoop/analytics.py) uses Apache Spark to perform distributed grouping and aggregation. It handles missing data (NaN) by calculating the mean of the specific domain.
3. **Registry (Output)**: A high-density JSON baseline file is generated at `hdfs/user/hadoop/processed/clinical_baselines.json`.
